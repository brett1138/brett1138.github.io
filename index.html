Brett C. Brinkmeyer

Creative

The dreams that come when we awake
Do not our entire picture make.
As we pass days idly by,
And the world is hurled across our eyes,

The streams around us, you and me,
Leave much untested and unseen.
Although we’d like to see it all,
Yet we stumble, yet we fall.

Slipping between our fingerprints,
The sands of time glitter and glint,
But who’s to wonder, and who’s to say
What makes us up, from day to day?

Together, we in our waking find
That dreaming’s caught us by the mind!
Marching toward whatever end;
Closer or farther, from our friends,
Holding fast and making sure
We’re somehow defined among the blur.

As the lines around us trace
(between the creases on our face)
We hold together, shining bright,
In waking, dreaming, through this night.

I cannot imagine the lives I have not lived,
Their potential shining just past the edge of my mind –

The paths I never walked, and the dazzling lights at their end
I can just now see my own, the light waiting for me
that path I have chosen with its broad spaces mixed with
tight turns of bramble-thorn.

The ambrosia of youth invades my steady gait, consistency fails.
Sadness, regret, pain, youth…

Mixed in the uncertainties and disappointments is the pulsing signal:
“You are here, we are here – together now we walk, we wait…”

The song goes on, though I am woefully out of tune (I never had perfect pitch,
and there are registers I can no longer hear), I will persist.

Despite the darkness I will share my light,
shedding light on the depths and darknesses infringing on the pains
the regrets,
the sadness,
and youth.

For the paths we walk, despite free will, are often not
wholly of our choosing.

Though we choose duty,
the shape of that duty
its size and scope
are beyond us.

I choose to step, step, and step again.
One foot at a time.

I will imagine the future, instead
it’s bring light shining toward me now
calling, beckoning, drawing,
until one day
when that recognition
becomes cognition

and our bleeding together
becomes healing, healed, whole.

We will breathe the ultimate sigh of relief,
which eternities cannot describe
of all the worries past and present
those tribulations-come-true
which rocked us
which derailed us
which changed the shape of our lives entirely

are memory, and their
resulting kinetics

grow

into the cracks they caused.
